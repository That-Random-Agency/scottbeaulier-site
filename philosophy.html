Vision & Approach

Building the future of business education.

Business schools should serve students and businesses. Great education should be affordable. And the best leaders are built through character, knowledge, and experience — not just textbooks.

Four Pillars

Guiding Principles
01

Serve Both Sides

The best programs serve students and the employers who will hire them. Academic ivory towers help no one.

02

Affordable Quality

Making degrees accessible doesn't mean cutting corners. It means innovating how education is delivered.

03

Get Off Campus

Innovation comes from listening — to students, to CEOs, to the marketplace. Not from committee meetings.

04

Character Matters

Tomorrow's leaders need more than hard skills. Character, experience, and ethical grounding are the foundation.

Academic degrees are a means to an end. Scott's focus is on maximizing each student's return on their investment and serving employers well by preparing graduates for a constantly changing, global economy. It's a formula that translates into flourishing for individuals and society.

Innovation in academic business programs comes from listening — to students and to employers. Scott makes it a priority to get off campus to meet with business leaders and apply those conversations to academic models. The result is curriculum that's grounded in real-world demands, not academic tradition for its own sake.

"While policies are integral, I believe that people — innovative, creative, and dedicated business leaders — are the lifeblood of any economy."

An economist by training, Scott has spent his career actively engaged in understanding and advocating for policies that foster dynamic, vibrant economies — the kind that best allow businesses and entrepreneurs to thrive. But, while policies are integral, he believes that people — innovative, creative, and dedicated business leaders — are the lifeblood of any economy.

Business schools have an enormous responsibility and role in preparing the next generation to change the world through business. It's a responsibility that Scott feels honored to have, and one that he doesn't take lightly. The next generation of leaders depends on educators to help set them up for success.

As the country struggles with how to address the skyrocketing cost of higher education, Scott is committed to being part of the solution. He has consistently proven that making academic degrees affordable and accessible does not mean sacrificing quality. It just requires responding to the modern marketplace, integrating the latest technologies, and not being afraid to drop the status quo.

Core Beliefs

  • Education is an investmentEvery student deserves a measurable return on their time and money.
  • Listen to employersCurriculum should reflect what the marketplace actually needs.
  • Affordable ≠ inferiorInnovation in delivery can reduce cost without reducing quality.
  • Character is curriculumEthics, experience, and leadership skills matter as much as technical knowledge.
  • Drop the status quoThe courage to change is the most important trait in higher ed leadership.
