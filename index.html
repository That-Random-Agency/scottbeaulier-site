Economist · Dean · University Leader

Institutional renewal, student opportunity, and the future of higher education.

University of Wyoming economist, educational entrepreneur, and university leader Scott Beaulier is focused on rethinking how higher education can better serve students, communities, and the public. As Dean of Business and Professor of Economics at the University of Wyoming, Scott brings together economics, leadership, innovation, and real-world problem solving to explore how universities can become stronger engines for opportunity, workforce development, and economic growth.

Known for making complex economic and educational issues accessible and relevant, Scott is a trusted voice on topics including the future of higher education, economic trends, leadership, innovation, and the evolving relationship between universities and the communities they serve. His work is grounded in the belief that education should do more than prepare students for careers. It should inspire ideas, strengthen communities, encourage entrepreneurship, and create lasting impact well beyond the classroom.

About Scott

Scott Beaulier is the H.A. (Dave) True Family Dean of Business and Professor of Economics at the University of Wyoming. An economist by training and an educational entrepreneur by practice, he focuses on how universities can be designed to better serve students, communities, and the public.

As dean, Beaulier oversees one of the nation's most distinctive public business schools, including nationally ranked online programs and a growing portfolio of industry partnerships, workforce initiatives, and philanthropic investments.

His leadership has included major fundraising efforts, accreditation reviews, and strategic initiatives centered on academic excellence, student opportunity, and strengthening public trust in higher education.
